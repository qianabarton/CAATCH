CAATCH
THIS!

CAATCH is a weeklong event to celebrate African American history and culture during Black History Month. This event will unite the many organizations working hard to make African American communities a better place, by building on the vision of Dr. Carter Woodson, the original creator of Black History Month.

to love ourselves.

CAATCH
US!

African Americans who observe Black history month usually have a great deal of self-love and appreciation of who they are.

CAATCH uses Black history month as the common denominator to unite the many organizations striving to create better communities. Many African American organizations and individuals have varying ideologies and philosophical viewpoints. However, what we have in common is the love of self. CAATCH also seeks to capture, and emphasize this sentiment.

WHO ARE WE?

Celebrating African Americans, the Culture, and History (CAATCH), Inc. was established as a 501(c)(3) organization in 2018 by founder and Executive Director, Orey Brockington III. CAATCH is headquartered in New York City, with a mission to create unity within the community by organizing the efforts of African Americans working within the community, raising awareness and funds for our prestige institutions, and celebrating the legacy and heritage of African Americans.
